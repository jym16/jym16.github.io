# The physical networks of neurons in the asymmetrical bodies of the Drosophila melanogaster brain

## Abstract

Physical networks, consisting of nodes and edges made of physical objects, are gaining interest due to their unique topological characteristics such as edge bundling and entanglement. The increasing availability of three-dimensional physical datasets has prompted interdisciplinary efforts to understand these systems. However, the question remains whether a physical network should be treated as a physical one, rather than a purely topological network, and if the physical layout of a physical network is redundant.

This short project examines the physical structures of neurons in the central brain of fruit fly *Drosophila melanogaster* [[1](#Scheffer2020connectome)], a large-scale empirical physical network with over 25,000 neurons and 20 million synapses. The study constructs the topological and physical networks of neurons in the brain region, focusing the asymmetrical bodies which play a role in sensory integration and motor control of the fruit fly. The study aims to explore the relation between the structures of the topological network and its physical layout.
